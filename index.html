How to Ad Test on FB

Predict the performance of your videos before posting them.

This is how you check how long people would watch your video for, and see exactly at which points they might drop off and stop watching.

You will need 3 main elements to create and Ad Test on Facebook:

  • Video
  • Title
  • Thumbnail

Here’s exactly what you need to do. No extra information.

If something isn’t mentioned there, you probably don’t need to touch it.

Go to adsmanager.facebook.com and select Campaigns from the menu on the left.

Under the Campaigns tab, click the green [+ Create] button

Select Engagement as the objective of the campaign

Select Manual engagement campaign

We will be working with 3 containers.

The first is the Campaign. Inside of it we have the Ad Set. Inside of that we have the Ad.

Let’s set up the Campaign first. All you need to do here is give your advertising campaign a unique name, that will not repeat with other campaigns you create.

Next, we’ll set up the Ad Set.

After you give your Ad Set a unique name, change the Conversion Location to On your ad.

Your settings under the Conversion section should end up looking like this.

Scroll down to the Budget & schedule section and select Lifetime budget.

Setting your budget between $3-$5 will be enough to help us achieve the result we want - 1,000 impressions.

Adjust the Start and End dates with the following notes in mind

  • They should be 24 hours apart
  • Adjust the starting time to have an extra 30 minutes to 1h from the current time. This provisions for processing time of the campaign and you uploading the videos you will test.
  • If you’re traveling, there may be a mismatch between your current time zone and the time zone you see when you set up the test.
  • ⚠️  After you set this up, double check that your budget has remained the same.

Scroll down to the Placements section and as you hover over the Advantage+ placements, an Edit button will appear on the right. Click it.

Select Manual placements.

Deselect all other platforms, except Facebook.

In Feeds select:

  • Facebook Feed
  • Facebook video feeds

In In-stream ads for reels select:

  • Facebook in-stream reels

You will only have your Ad Set showing in 3 placements.

We’re ready to move to the final container and give a unique name to our Ad.

If you’re working with many Facebook pages, select the one you’d like to create the test for under the Identity section of the Ad container.

The fun part! Scroll down to the Ad Creative section and click the [Video ad] button

This is your Media library. It’s like a folder of videos that will stay in the Ads manager platform. You can upload multiple videos at the same time here.

Go ahead and click the [Upload] button at the top right. Once the videos are uploaded from your computer into the Media library, select the video that you will test and hit [Next]

The next steps on the left are about tweaking your video. You have probably already done that in the edit, so you can skip most of them. The one you want to look into is Text.

This is where you will write the Title that you want to test for your video.

The last, Enhancements, step will suggest visual touch ups and AI enhancements to your creative (video). Let’s keep those off, so that we test something that will be as close as possible to the actual video you will post.

Now that you can see your video in the Ad creative section, we’re going to select a thumbnail.

Click [Edit Media], then [Edit Video]

On the left side of this pop-up screen, select Thumbnail.

Then, select (Manual).

You can either choose one of the suggested thumbnails, or hit the [Upload] button and add your own image.

Don’t forget to click [Save] at the bottom right.

You can stop here, hit [Publish] and move on.

But our Pro Creators would test variations in the 3 main elements (Video, Title, Thumbnail) to see the difference in performance.

The best element to test variations for is your video. You can test different edits of the same video. The opening shot is the most impactful thing to the performance - try editing and testing different versions of that with priority.

You can also change the Title and the Thumbnail and test how that affects performance. Usually we see a much lower impact than the video.

Alright, this is how you’ll create the next variation in your test.

Click the three dots next to the Ad Set and select Duplicate.

On the next screen, choose to duplicate it under the Existing, Original campaign.

Now you have two Ad Sets under the same Campaign.

Let’s give the second Ad Set and the second Ad a unique name.

Next, you’ll click into the second Ad and scroll to the Ad Creative section.

Click [Edit Media], then [Change Video] and add the second video you will test. You can follow the same process as above.

You can duplicate the Ad Set and change the video as many times as you want by following the same steps. Most creators usually test 2 or 3 Ad Sets at a time.

Once you’re done setting everything up, click back into the Campaign container at the top left. This will make sure your whole campaign gets published.

Finally, click the green [Publish] button at the bottom right.
