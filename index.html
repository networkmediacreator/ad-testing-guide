How to Ad Test on Facebook

Predict the performance of your videos before posting them. This guide shows you how to check how long people would watch your video for, and see exactly at which points they might drop off and stop watching.

1

Access Facebook Ads Manager

Go to adsmanager.facebook.com and select Campaigns from the menu on the left.

2

Create New Campaign

Under the Campaigns tab, click the green [+ Create] button to start creating your new ad campaign.

3

Select Campaign Objective

Select Engagement as the objective of the campaign. This objective is specifically designed for testing video performance and engagement metrics.

4

Choose Campaign Type

Select Manual engagement campaign to have full control over your ad testing parameters.

5

Campaign Setup

We'll be working with 3 containers: the Campaign, inside of it the Ad Set, and inside of that the Ad. Give your advertising campaign a unique name that won't repeat with other campaigns you create.

6

Ad Set Configuration

After giving your Ad Set a unique name, change the Conversion Location to On your ad.

Your settings under the Conversion section should look like this:

7

Budget & Schedule

Scroll down to the Budget & schedule section and select Lifetime budget.

Setting your budget between $3-$5 will be enough to help us achieve the result we want - 1,000 impressions.

Schedule Guidelines

  • Start and End dates should be 24 hours apart
  • Adjust the starting time to have an extra 30 minutes to 1 hour from the current time for processing
  • If traveling, check for time zone mismatches

8

Configure Placements

Scroll down to the Placements section. Hover over Advantage+ placements and click the Edit button that appears.

Select Manual placements for precise control over where your ads appear.

Deselect all other platforms except Facebook.

Feed Selection

  • Facebook Feed
  • Facebook video feeds

In-stream Ads for Reels

  • Facebook in-stream reels

You will only have your Ad Set showing in 3 placements.

9

Ad Creative Setup

Give your Ad a unique name to distinguish it from other ads in your campaign.

If you're working with multiple Facebook pages, select the appropriate one under the Identity section.

Scroll down to the Ad Creative section and click the [Video ad] button.

10

Upload Video & Set Thumbnail

This is your Media library - a folder of videos that will stay in the Ads manager platform. You can upload multiple videos at the same time here. Click the [Upload] button at the top right. Once uploaded, select your video and hit [Next].

The next steps are about tweaking your video. You can skip most of them since you've likely already edited your video. Focus on the Text section to add your title.

This is where you'll write the Title that you want to test for your video.

In the Enhancements step, keep AI enhancements off so you test something as close as possible to your actual video.

To set a custom thumbnail, click [Edit Media], then [Edit Video].

Select Thumbnail on the left, then choose (Manual). You can either choose from suggested thumbnails or upload your own image using the [Upload] button.

Don't forget to click [Save] at the bottom right.

11

Create Testing Variations

You can stop here and hit [Publish], but Pro Creators test variations in the 3 main elements (Video, Title, Thumbnail) to see performance differences.

Pro Creator Tip

The best element to test variations for is your video. Test different edits of the same video. The opening shot is the most impactful - prioritize testing different versions of that. Title and thumbnail changes usually have lower impact than video variations.

To create variations, click the three dots next to the Ad Set and select Duplicate.

Choose to duplicate it under the existing, original campaign.

Give the second Ad Set and Ad unique names.

Click into the second Ad, scroll to Ad Creative, click [Edit Media], then [Change Video] to add your second test video.

You can duplicate the Ad Set and change videos as many times as needed. Most creators test 2-3 Ad Sets at a time.

12

Publish Your Campaign

Once you're done setting everything up, click back into the Campaign container at the top left to ensure your whole campaign gets published.

Finally, click the green [Publish] button at the bottom right to launch your ad test campaign.
