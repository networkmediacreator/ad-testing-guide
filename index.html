How to Ad Test on Facebook

A practical guide to designing and running Facebook ad tests

Introduction

Predict the performance of your videos before posting them. This guide will help you set up effective ad tests on Facebook and understand how long people watch your video and where they drop off.

You'll need three core elements to run a test:

  • Video: The creative itself—what your audience will watch.
  • Title: A headline that grabs attention and sets expectations.
  • Thumbnail: The image that entices viewers to press play.

If something isn't mentioned in this guide, you probably don't need to touch it. We'll focus only on what matters for an efficient test.

Step 1: Create Your Campaign

Select Engagement as your campaign objective in Meta Ads Manager. Engagement campaigns are great for collecting messages, video views, post engagement, Page likes, and event responses.

Under campaign settings:

  • Pick a unique name for your campaign that clearly describes the test.
  • Use the default buying type (Auction) unless you have a specific reason to change it.
  • Ensure "Engagement" is selected as the objective.

Keep things simple—don't worry about optional settings like split tests or dynamic creative at this stage.

Step 2: Configure Your Ad Set

The ad set is where you choose your audience and placements. Start by naming your ad set something descriptive (e.g., VideoTest_Audience1).

Key settings:

  • Conversion Location: Set to On your ad to count engagement directly on the ad.
  • Engagement Type: Choose Video views to optimise for video play time.
  • Performance Goal: Use ThruPlay views to maximise the number of viewers who watch at least 15 seconds.
  • Budget: Select a Lifetime budget. A budget of $3–$5 per ad set is typically enough to achieve around 1,000 impressions.
  • Schedule: Choose start and end dates that are at least 24 hours apart. Add an extra hour after your current time to account for ad review and upload time.

Step 3: Select Placements

Under the Placements section, hover over Advantage+ placements and click Edit. Select Manual placements and enable only the Facebook feed. This ensures your test has consistent placement and reduces variables.

Leave other delivery settings at their defaults to keep the test straightforward.

Step 4: Design Your Ads

Within each ad set, create one ad for each creative variation you want to test. Change only one element at a time so you can accurately measure its effect.

  • Video: Test different video cuts, intros, or overlays.
  • Title: Experiment with headlines that emphasise benefits, curiosity, or urgency.
  • Thumbnail: Try contrasting colours, faces, or icons to see what catches attention best.

Keep the call-to-action consistent across all ads. Use succinct copy and avoid changes to targeting between creatives.

Step 5: Analyse and Iterate

Once your test completes, review the results in Ads Manager. Compare metrics like ThruPlay cost, watch time, and click-through rate (CTR) across variations.

Scale the top-performing creative by increasing its budget or duplicating the ad into a new campaign. Pause underperforming ads, and apply insights to future tests.

Repeat the process, experimenting with new ideas while keeping one variable at a time. Systematic testing helps you continuously improve your ads and maximise ROI.
